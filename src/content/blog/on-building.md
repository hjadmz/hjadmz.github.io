The best things I've built started with a constraint. Not inspiration. Not a blank canvas. A **box** — tight, defined, uncomfortable — and the process of finding elegance within those walls.

## Constraints as fuel

A monochrome palette forces hierarchy through spacing and weight, not color. A 14-day deadline forces you to cut scope to the bone and ship what matters.

> "Architecture is the learned game, correct and magnificent, of forms assembled in the light." — Le Corbusier

## Compounding decisions

Every small decision compounds. The font weight. The transition timing. The negative space around a heading. Individually, invisible. Together, the difference between something that *works* and something that doesn't.

Ship it. Then refine.
